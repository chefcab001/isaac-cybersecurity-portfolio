1. Most Time-Sensitive Item

Priority action

CVE-2026-56164 — Microsoft SharePoint Server, missing authentication for a critical function.

Federal remediation due date: July 17, 2026 — effectively no runway left. Unauthenticated, zero-click, network-reachable, and already exploited. Microsoft's own CVSS score (5.3, Moderate) badly understates the operational risk — NVD independently scored it 9.8 Critical. If you run on-premises SharePoint Server 2016, 2019, or Subscription Edition, this is today's priority, not this week's.

CVSS score alone should not drive prioritization. NIST guidance is explicit that exploit maturity and confirmed active exploitation should override base CVSS scores in patch sequencing — directly relevant to the SharePoint entry above, where a 5.3 score would otherwise bury an urgent, actively exploited fix behind dozens of higher-scored but non-exploited items.

4. Methodology

Step 1 — Primary source. CISA, NVD, or the vendor's own advisory. This is the authoritative record and takes precedence whenever sources disagree.

Step 2 — Independent industry corroboration. Established security research and reporting is checked to confirm the primary-source reading matches how the broader GRC and vulnerability-management community is interpreting the same data. This step never overrides the primary source; it's a sanity check against misreading it.

Where a claim exists only in an aggregator or newsletter digest and can't be traced to a primary advisory, it's excluded or explicitly labeled as an unverified secondary source. This two-step check is what caught the July 14 KEV gap referenced above — it stays part of the process for every brief published under this name.
