1. Most Time-Sensitive Item

Priority Action

CVE-2025-5777 — Citrix NetScaler ADC / Gateway ("CitrixBleed 2"). Already in the CISA KEV Catalog since July 2025, and CISA's own catalog lists this entry's known ransomware use as "Known," a stronger designation than anything newly added this week. Huntress independently confirmed this week that an initial access broker is chaining this vulnerability through privilege escalation into DragonForce ransomware deployment, six confirmed intrusions across unrelated organizations in the first half of 2026. If you run internet-facing Citrix NetScaler and haven't confirmed this patch and rotated sessions, treat it as equal or higher priority than anything in the table below, regardless of its older KEV date.

4. Emerging Threats

"Helix" data-extortion group

ReliaQuest disclosed a previously unreported extortion group, tracked as Helix, running an identity-driven campaign against Microsoft 365 environments. A first compromised identity is used via vishing and device-code phishing to quietly enumerate and bulk-download SharePoint document libraries over several days; a second, separately compromised identity is used later, sometimes weeks later, solely to deliver the extortion demand internally over Teams and email. ReliaQuest notes possible technique and infrastructure overlap with the BlackFile and ShinyHunters extortion ecosystem, its own assessment, not independently corroborated here.

Zimbra Classic Web Client — stored XSS

Zimbra shipped ZCS 10.1.19 on July 7, 2026, fixing a stored cross-site scripting flaw in the Classic Web Client where a crafted email could execute script in a victim's session. No CVE identifier has been assigned by the vendor as of this check. Organizations still on Classic Web Client should patch or move affected users to the unaffected Modern UI.

Second confirmed campaign: Anubis ransomware also exploiting CitrixBleed 2

Independent of the Huntress-tracked DragonForce chain in Section 1, Arctic Wolf reported a separate set of intrusions in which Anubis ransomware affiliates are independently exploiting CVE-2025-5777, alongside valid VPN credential abuse, then escalating through legitimate RMM tools (ScreenConnect, Zoho Assist, MeshAgent, Remotely, UltraVNC) to blend in with normal IT activity. Per The Hacker News, citing Ransomware.Live data not independently opened this session, Anubis has claimed 91 victims to date, 11 in June 2026 alone, concentrated in healthcare, business services, manufacturing, technology, and financial services, more than half U.S.-based. Anubis also carries an irreversible data-wiping module that reduces files to 0 KB regardless of ransom payment.

5. Active Nation-State Threat Campaign

CISA, NSA, FBI, and DC3, joined by 14 international partner agencies, released a joint advisory (AA26-194A) on July 14 attributing global targeting of vulnerable public-facing networking devices to Russian FSB Center 16, with emphasis on communications, defense industrial base, energy, financial services, government, and healthcare sectors.

7. Methodology

Step 1, Primary source. CISA, NVD, or the vendor's/researcher's own advisory. This is the authoritative record and takes precedence whenever sources disagree.

Step 2, Independent corroboration. Broader reporting is checked to confirm the primary-source reading matches how the security community is interpreting the same data. This never overrides the primary source, it's a sanity check.

Where a claim exists only in an aggregator and can't be traced to a primary source, it's excluded or explicitly labeled unverified, see Section 3's "Moderate" and "Unconfirmed" entries above.

8. Confidence Summary

HIGH confidence on all ten KEV entries, the CitrixBleed 2 / DragonForce chain, the CitrixBleed 2 / Anubis campaign, the Helix group, and the Zimbra XSS flaw, each checked directly against its own primary source this session. MODERATE confidence on the specific CVE usage figures inside the WP-SHELLSTORM campaign and on the Anubis victim count (91 claimed, 11 in June 2026), both sourced through a secondary outlet citing named research or third-party tracking data rather than opened directly. LOW confidence, unconfirmed, on any attribution of SharePoint exploitation to the Storm-2603 actor; it is not included as a finding above pending direct verification.
