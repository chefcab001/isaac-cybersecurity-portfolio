Executive Summary

This project demonstrates a public-safe security review methodology for an AI-enabled platform concept. The published version excludes sensitive implementation details and focuses on methodology, risk thinking, evidence handling, and governance communication.

Assessment Scope

Authentication and session assurance

Review of identity assurance concepts and session control expectations.

Authorization and tenant isolation concepts

High-level analysis of role separation and isolation boundaries.

Secure architecture review

Sanitized review of security design patterns and control coverage.

AI and LLM governance considerations

Governance awareness for prompt handling, model use, and data exposure risk.

Audit logging and evidence readiness

Readiness concepts for traceability, review, and compliance discussion.

Risk prioritization and executive communication

Translation of technical observations into leadership-ready language.

DevSecOps and secure change control

Change control and publication discipline supporting secure delivery.

Methodology

Inventory → Threat Model → Control Mapping → Evidence Review → Risk Rating → Recommendations → Executive Summary

Public-Safe Findings Summary

Identity and Access Governance

Observed Theme: Identity and session assurance expectations.

Potential Risk: Inconsistent access governance can weaken trust.

Recommended Control Direction: Strengthen identity lifecycle and session controls.

Tenant Isolation Assurance

Observed Theme: Multi-tenant separation concepts.

Potential Risk: Boundary weaknesses can increase data exposure concerns.

Recommended Control Direction: Reinforce isolation and role separation validation.

Audit and Monitoring Readiness

Observed Theme: Logging and traceability expectations.

Potential Risk: Limited observability can slow review and response.

Recommended Control Direction: Improve logging coverage and evidence readiness.

AI Governance and Data Handling

Observed Theme: AI and LLM usage governance.

Potential Risk: Unclear handling rules can increase data leakage concerns.

Recommended Control Direction: Apply prompt governance and data minimization.

Secure Development Lifecycle

Observed Theme: Change control and secure delivery discipline.

Potential Risk: Weak review gates can introduce avoidable risk.

Recommended Control Direction: Enforce secure SDLC controls and release review.

Executive Risk Reporting

Observed Theme: Leadership-oriented communication.

Potential Risk: Technical-only reporting can obscure business impact.

Recommended Control Direction: Translate findings into actionable executive language.

Evidence Governance
